# Write a business email

**Goal:** Send a clear email that gets a reply.

## What you need (inputs)
- Who you are
- Recipient role
- Context (1–3 bullets)
- Desired outcome (one sentence)

## Copy‑paste prompt templates
**Prompt 1 — Draft email**

```text
Write a business email.
Tone: neutral, respectful.
Goal: <goal>.
Constraints: short, no fluff, one clear CTA.
Output: Subject + Email body.
Context:
<<<CONTEXT>>>
```

**Prompt 2 — Follow-up**

```text
Write a polite follow-up to the previous email. Keep it under 80 words. Add one question.
Previous email:
<<<EMAIL>>>
```

**Prompt 3 — Negotiation (scope)**

```text
Rewrite this email to clearly define scope and prevent endless revisions.
Add: what is included / not included, and next step.
Email:
<<<EMAIL>>>
```


## Quality check (30 seconds)
- Subject matches the ask
- One CTA only
- No vague language
- All facts are correct

## Common mistakes
- Multiple asks in one email
- Passive tone (no CTA)
- Too long for busy people
